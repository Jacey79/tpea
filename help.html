Introduction to TPEA

Pain is a complex sensory experience shaped by injury, inflammation, and nervous-system plasticity across peripheral and central sites. Understanding the molecular basis of pain requires integrating evidence from high-throughput transcriptomics, targeted wet-lab validation, and functional perturbation studies. Each of these illuminates a different facet of how genes contribute to pain.

TPEA (The Pain Expression Atlas) is a comprehensive resource integrating 13,922 pain-associated gene records, curated across 7 pain-model categories, 17 pain-model sub-categories, and 4 species groups. Each record is annotated with three independent lines of evidence (Validated perturbation, Validated differential expression, and High-throughput profiling) and summarized by a unified Confidence score. Rich pain-model descriptions, seminal references, and cross-linked filters let users navigate from gene to pain model to tissue to evidence in a single session.

Field dictionary

FieldDescription
TPEA IDUnique identifier for each TPEA entry. Combines a gene, species, tissue, and pain-model context into a single primary key (e.g. TPEA_00001).
Gene symbolHGNC / MGI / RGD approved gene symbol.
Ensembl IDEnsembl gene stable ID (ENSG / ENSMUSG / ENSRNOG prefix).
SpeciesSpecies grouping: Human, Mouse, Rat, or Others.
SourceTissue or cell line from which evidence was derived.
Pain model categoryBroad class of pain model (7 total, e.g. Neuropathic Pain Models).
Pain model sub-categorySpecific paradigm (e.g. CCI, SNI, CFA).
High-throughput profilingNumber of supporting high-throughput profiling datasets (bulk RNA-seq, miRNA-seq, microarray) reporting differential expression of this gene in this context. Discovery-tier evidence.
Validated differential expressionNumber of supporting hypothesis-driven differential-expression studies (qPCR / Western / IHC / IF / ELISA) documented in the literature. Mid-tier evidence.
Validated perturbationNumber of supporting perturbation studies (KO, cKO, siRNA, AAV-shRNA, overexpression, pharmacology, antibody neutralization) reporting a measurable change in pain behavior or sensitivity. Highest-tier (causal) evidence.
Confidence scoreSum of Validated perturbation + Validated differential expression + High-throughput profiling evidence counts. Higher score indicates stronger and more diverse evidence of pain association.

FAQ

Can I download the full dataset?

Yes. See the Download page. All data are packaged as zip archives of tab-separated files.

Can I cite TPEA in my publication?

Yes. Please see the Citation page for the current recommended citation and BibTeX.

How is the Confidence score calculated?

Confidence score = Validated perturbation count + Validated differential expression count + High-throughput profiling count. It is a simple additive summary rather than a weighted statistic, so two entries with the same score may have very different underlying evidence distributions. Always inspect the per-tier counts for downstream prioritization.
